### Category Creation Strategy Framework

**What is "category creation"?**
Category creation means being the first to:
1. Name a problem (or solution) that didn't have a name
2. Position yourself as the default choice for that category
3. Make competitors "the alternative to" rather than "another option in"

**The "RTO Enrollment AI" category:**
- Problem: RTOs spend 25-60 hours/week on enrollment calls, missing 20-40% of inquiries
- Solution: AI-powered automation of enrollment calls (24/7, ASQA-compliant)
- Category: "RTO Enrollment AI" — AI specifically designed for Australian RTO enrollment operations

**Why this category needs to be created (not claimed):**
- "Voice AI" exists but isn't RTO-specific
- "Enrollment software" exists but isn't AI-powered
- "AI for education" is too broad
- "RTO AI" is the whitespace

### Build vs. Claim Framework

**"Build" vs. "Claim" category:**

| Approach | Pros | Cons | When to Use |
|----------|------|------|-------------|
| **Build** (create new term) | Strong differentiation, defensible | Slower adoption, education required | NOW (when category doesn't exist) |
| **Claim** (own existing term) | Faster, existing demand | Crowded, less defensible | When category already exists |

**Decision for Optimizer AI:**
- Build "RTO Enrollment AI" as new category (recommended)
- Claim "AI for RTOs" as adjacent category (secondary)
- Avoid "voice AI" or "AI for education" (too crowded, too generic)
