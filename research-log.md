### RTO CEO Buyer Persona

**Name**: "The Visionary Operator"
**Role**: CEO, Owner, Managing Director
**Age**: 40-60
**Location**: Regional QLD/NSW (60%), metro (40%)
**Company size**: 20-200 students/month
**Experience**: 10+ years in vocational education, often ex-TAFE or industry background
**Technology comfort**: Medium (uses Zoho, email, social media; not technical)

**Day in the life**:
- 6:00 AM: Check emails, respond to urgent (early riser)
- 7:00 AM: Commute or gym
- 8:30 AM: Arrive office, review overnight inquiries (leads in Zoho)
- 9:00 AM: Team standup, enrollment pipeline review
- 10:00 AM: Sales call with prospective student OR compliance review
- 11:30 AM: Marketing review (Google Ads, lead quality)
- 12:00 PM: Lunch (often working)
- 1:00 PM: Enrollment calls (peak call time)
- 3:00 PM: Student issues, complaints, complex situations
- 4:30 PM: End-of-day admin, review metrics
- 5:30 PM: Leave office (but check phone at home)

**Biggest pain points** (in order):
1. **"We can't keep up with inquiry volume"** — Staff burned out, calls missed, students go elsewhere
2. **"I don't know which marketing works"** — Millions spent, no clear attribution
3. **"Compliance is eating my time"** — ASQA audits, documentation, policy updates
4. **"Finding good staff is impossible"** — Enrollment staff turnover, training costs
5. **"Revenue is flat"** — Can't grow without adding headcount

**Purchase triggers**:
- Staff resignation/turnover (immediate need)
- ASQA audit warning (compliance panic)
- Competitor using AI (fear of missing out)
- Seasonal volume spike (enrollment period overload)
- Referral from trusted peer (trust transfer)

**Purchase blockers**:
- "I need to think about it" (analysis paralysis)
- "My staff won't use it" (change resistance)
- "The last AI tool failed" (past trauma)
- "I don't have time to implement" (bandwidth)
- "What's the ROI?" (justification need)

**Decision-making process**:
1. Problem recognition (internal or peer reference)
2. Research (Google, LinkedIn, RTO Connect)
3. Vendor shortlist (2-3 options)
4. Demo (30-min call with CEO or manager)
5. Reference check (call another customer)
6. Decision (often sole decision-maker for <$20K/year)

**Influencers**:
- Peer RTO CEOs (trusted)
- RTO Connect community (moderately trusted)
- Sales reps (low trust initially, builds over time)
- Google reviews (low trust, but researched)
- Partners (Zoho consultants, RTO advisors)

**Media consumption**:
- LinkedIn: Daily (30 min morning + 15 min evening)
- Email: Constant (work email, checks 10+ times/day)
- Podcasts: Industry podcasts (VET Voice, RTO Connect podcast)
- YouTube: Rare (too busy)
- Facebook: Rare (personal only)
- News: ABC, The Australian (political/policy news)
- Conferences: RTO Connect Forum (annual), state RTO networks

**Objection language** (how they express resistance):
- "AI will never work for our students"
- "We tried [competitor] and it was terrible"
- "My enrollment team is doing fine"
- "I'm not convinced this is worth the cost"
- "I need to talk to my business partner first"

**What they need to hear**:
- Peer success story (RTO like theirs)
- Specific ROI ("saves 20 hours/week, recovers 10% more inquiries")
- Easy implementation ("we handle everything, 1-week setup")
- Compliance assurance ("ASQA-compliant, built for RTOs")

**Personal motivation**:
- Build a business that runs without them (freedom)
- reputation in industry (pride)
- Help students get jobs (mission)
- Beat competitors (competition)

### Enrollment Manager Buyer Persona

**Name**: "The Overwhelmed Coordinator"
**Role**: Enrollment Manager, Student Services Manager, Operations Manager
**Age**: 28-45
**Location**: Metro (60%), regional (40%)
**Company size**: 50-200 students/month
**Experience**: 3-7 years in enrollment/student services, often from customer service background
**Reports to**: CEO or Operations Director
**Technology comfort**: High (Zoho, spreadsheets, automation; curious about AI)

**Day in the life**:
- 8:00 AM: Arrive, check overnight missed calls, review voicemail
- 8:30 AM: Respond to email inquiries (often 20-30/day)
- 9:00 AM: Team briefing (what's in pipeline, issues)
- 9:30 AM: Make outbound calls to unconverted leads
- 11:00 AM: Process enrollments (paperwork, USI collection, orientation booking)
- 12:30 PM: Lunch (rarely full hour)
- 1:00 PM: Orientation session (1-2 hrs, leads into enrollment)
- 3:00 PM: Urgent calls, student issues, rescheduling
- 4:30 PM: Admin (update Zoho, follow up on pending enrollments)
- 5:00 PM: Leave (but takes work home mentally)

**Biggest pain points** (in order):
1. **"I'm drowning in calls"** — 100+ calls/week, can't get admin done
2. **"People don't show up to orientation"** — 20-30% no-show rate, wasted time
3. **"USI collection is a nightmare"** — Students don't know what it is, drop out
4. **"I answer the same questions 50 times a day"** — Price, schedule, requirements
5. **"My boss expects growth but no extra staff"** — Do more with same team

**Purchase triggers**:
- Peak enrollment period (term start, funding cycle)
- Staff sick leave (panic mode)
- Boss breathing down neck (metrics pressure)
- Peer mentions AI tool (FOMO)
- New course launch (volume spike)

**Purchase blockers**:
- "Will my team accept this?" (change anxiety)
- "What if AI gives wrong information?" (quality fear)
- "I'm the one who has to manage it" (workload)
- "Students might complain" (political)
- "Will it actually save me time?" (skepticism)

**Decision-making process**:
1. Problem recognized (daily frustration)
2. Research for solution (Google, LinkedIn)
3. Proposal to boss (recommendation)
4. Demo (often attends with CEO)
5. Implementation planning (owns the rollout)
6. Day-to-day use and evaluation (decides if it works)

**Influencers**:
- CEO (final approval, but recommendation matters)
- Peers at other RTOs (highly trusted)
- Vendor demos (persuasive)
- Case studies (evidence)
- Team members (adoption feasibility)

**Media consumption**:
- LinkedIn: Daily (30-60 min, personal + professional)
- Email: Work email (constant)
- Instagram: Personal use (mornings, evenings)
- TikTok: Rare (personal, 15 min/day)
- Podcasts: Business/motivation (gym, commute)
- YouTube: How-to videos (tech tools, productivity)
- RTO Connect Forum: Monthly (online community)

**Objection language**:
- "My students won't talk to a robot"
- "I already have Zoho — isn't that enough?"
- "AI can't handle our complex funding questions"
- "I'm too busy to train my team on new software"
- "What happens when AI makes a mistake?"

**What they need to hear**:
- Specific time savings ("You'll get back 5 hours/week")
- Quality assurance ("AI transfers to you for anything complex")
- Easy training ("Your team learns in 15 minutes")
- Student experience ("Students actually prefer 24/7 availability")
- Quick wins ("First week you'll see results")

**Personal motivation**:
- Look good to boss (performance recognition)
- Reduce stress (work-life balance)
- Build skills (career progression)
- Help students succeed (mission)
- Prove AI works (innovation advocate)

### Marketing Manager Buyer Persona

**Name**: "The Attribution-Hungry Analyst"
**Role**: Marketing Manager, Digital Marketing Coordinator, Growth Manager
**Age**: 25-40
**Location**: Metro (80%), regional (20%)
**Company size**: 50-200 students/month (often in larger RTOs)
**Experience**: 2-5 years in digital marketing, often agency background
**Reports to**: CEO or Marketing Director
**Technology comfort**: Very high (Google Ads, Meta, analytics, automation tools)

**Day in the life**:
- 8:30 AM: Review dashboard (GA4, Google Ads, leads in Zoho)
- 9:30 AM: Optimize campaigns (bid adjustments, audience tweaks)
- 11:00 AM: Content creation (social posts, blog, ads)
- 12:30 PM: Lunch
- 1:30 PM: Team meeting (marketing strategy)
- 2:00 PM: Review lead quality (why are leads dropping off?)
- 3:30 PM: Report to CEO (what's working, what's not)
- 4:30 PM: Plan tomorrow's campaign tweaks
- 5:30 PM: Leave (but often checking phone)

**Biggest pain points** (in order):
1. **"I don't know which channels convert"** — Working in dark, can't prove value
2. **"Leads come from everywhere but enrollments don't"** — Attribution gap
3. **"Marketing gets blamed for everything"** — CEO expects leads, doesn't understand funnel
4. **"There's no budget for better tools"** — DIY with spreadsheets
5. **"Our website doesn't capture intent"** — High traffic, low conversion

**Purchase triggers**:
- CEO asks for marketing ROI report (accountability pressure)
- New campaign budget request (needs justification)
- Competitor analysis (find better tools)
- Industry benchmark review (see what works)
- Marketing conference (learn new approaches)

**Purchase blockers**:
- "We don't have budget for another tool"
- "I already have Google Analytics — isn't that enough?"
- "Attribution is too complex for us"
- "We need a full strategy, not just a dashboard"
- "This will take months to implement"

**Decision-making process**:
1. Problem recognized (attribution gap)
2. Research solutions (articles, tools, consultants)
3. Shortlist (2-3 options)
4. Demo (solo or with CEO)
5. Proposal to CEO (budget justification)
6. Implementation (owns the setup)

**Influencers**:
- Google/YouTube tutorials (technical research)
- Marketing podcasts (trends)
- Agency partners (recommendations)
- LinkedIn thought leaders (attribution experts)
- CEO (budget approval)

**Media consumption**:
- LinkedIn: Daily (1+ hour, industry news)
- YouTube: Daily (tutorials, marketing channels like Alex Chen)
- Podcasts: Marketing podcasts (Marketing Against the Grain, Marketing School)
- Twitter/X: Industry discussions
- Blogs: Marketing articles (Search Engine Journal, HubSpot)
- Email: Marketing newsletters (Morning Brew, industry)

**Objection language**:
- "How does this connect to Zoho?"
- "What about phone call attribution?"
- "I've tried attribution before — it's always wrong"
- "We need multi-touch, not last-click"
- "This won't work for RTOs — we're different"

**What they need to hear**:
- Zoho integration (data flows automatically)
- Phone call attribution ("We track every channel, including calls")
- ASQA audit trail ("Marketing data retained for 3 years")
- RTO-specific metrics ("Enrollment source, cost per enrollment")
- Quick implementation ("Connected in 2 hours, data flowing by end of day")

**Personal motivation**:
- Prove marketing value (job security)
- Build impressive dashboards (pride)
- Learn new skills (career growth)
- Get budget increase (influence)
- Be seen as strategic partner (influence)

### Persona-Specific Outreach Messaging

**For CEO** (Visionary Operator):
- Hook: "60+ hours/week on enrollment calls is killing your team. Here's how RTOs are cutting that to 6."
- ROI: "For $999/month, a 100-student RTO saves $12,500/month in staff time + recovers $5,000/month in lost inquiries."
- Credibility: "Hader Institute (QLD RTO) went from 25 hours/week to 6. Same staff, more enrollments."
- Compliance: "ASQA-compliant, 3-year call retention, audit trail built in."
- Close: "15-minute demo. No commitment. See your ROI before you pay."

**For Enrollment Manager** (Overwhelmed Coordinator):
- Hook: "Stop answering the same 5 questions 50 times a day. AI handles the routine so you handle the enrollments."
- Time: "You'll get back 15+ hours/week to focus on students who actually need you."
- Quality: "AI transfers to you for anything complex. Your team reviews, approves, completes."
- Ease: "We handle the setup. Your staff is running in 1 week."
- Close: "Let's show you how it works. 15 minutes, no tech skills required."

**For Marketing Manager** (Attribution-Hungry Analyst):
- Hook: "If you can't answer 'which channel drives enrollments,' you're flying blind. Here's the fix."
- Data: "Multi-touch attribution from first-touch to enrollment. Every channel, every campaign."
- Zoho: "Data flows from Google Ads → Zoho → Dashboard. No manual export."
- ASQA: "Marketing records retained for 3 years. Audit-ready reports with one click."
- Close: "See exactly which $1 of ad spend generates $5 of enrollment revenue."
