### Australian AI Regulatory Landscape — Current State (May 2026)

**The regulatory context**:
Australia has historically taken a voluntary/self-regulatory approach to AI. The AI Safety Summit (November 2023) resulted in non-binding principles ("Safe and Responsible AI"). However, by late 2025, the federal government signaled a shift toward mandatory requirements following EU AI Act implementation and increasing community concern about AI risks.

**Key documents and positions**:

| Document | Status | Key Requirements |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **AI Safety Institute Act 2025** | Passed Dec 2025 | Establishes voluntary AI safety evaluations |
| **NSW AI Assurance Framework** | Voluntary, Oct 2025 | Risk-based AI governance for government agencies |
| **National AI Governance Framework** | Discussion paper, Feb 2026 | Voluntary principles + potential mandatory in future |
| **EU AI Act (for reference)** | In effect Dec 2024 | Sets global precedent, affects companies operating in EU |

**Current Australian AI regulatory approach** (May 2026):
- **Voluntary** for most AI deployments
- **Mandatory** for high-risk AI (healthcare, critical infrastructure, government)
- **Contemplated** for employment decisions, credit decisions, and education
- **Expected**: Mandatory requirements for "high-risk AI systems" by mid-2026

### What "High-Risk AI" Means for RTO Enrollment

**EU AI Act classification (reference for AU direction)**:
The EU AI Act categorizes education as "high-risk" because AI in education can significantly impact career opportunities and access to services.

**EU AI Act high-risk requirements include**:
- Transparency (AI must disclose it's AI)
- Human oversight (humans can override AI decisions)
- Data governance (training data quality)
- Accuracy and robustness
- Record-keeping (audit trails)

**Australian likely direction** (based on government papers):
Education AI is explicitly mentioned in National AI Governance Framework discussion paper as an area requiring attention. The most likely outcome:
- Mandatory disclosure: AI systems in education must disclose they're AI
- Human oversight: Education enrollment AI requires human review pathway
- Documentation: AI systems must maintain decision records
- No ban on AI enrollment — but with compliance requirements
