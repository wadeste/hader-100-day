### Australian RTO Enrollment Cycles

**National VET Enrollment Patterns:**

Based on NCVER data and RTO industry patterns:

| Period | Enrollment Volume | RTO Staff Capacity | Decision-Making |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Jan-Feb** | VERY HIGH | CRISIS mode (intake processing) | Frozen — can't buy anything |
| **March** | HIGH | Still busy (ramp-down) | Emerging — first decisions |
| **April-June** | MEDIUM | Normal operations | OPEN — evaluating tools |
| **July-August** | HIGH (semester 2) | Busy again (intake) | Frozen — no new initiatives |
| **Sept-October** | HIGH | Busy (semester 2 intake) | Delayed decisions |

**Primary enrollment periods:**
- **Semester 1:** January-February (peak)
- **Semester 2:** July-August (peak)

**Secondary enrollment periods:**
- Rolling enrollment (some RTOs year-round)
- Quarterly intakes (particularly for short courses)
- Apprenticeship/traineeship (continuous, less seasonal)

### Qualification-Specific Timing

**Cert IV / Diploma Courses (primary RTO focus):**
- Peak intake: January-February, July-August
- Decision window: March-April, September-October
- Optimal sales timing: **March-April** (semester 1 decisions), **September-October** (semester 2 decisions)

**Apprenticeships / Traineeships:**
- Rolling enrollment (continuous, less seasonal)
- Decision timing: Year-round, but Q2 and Q4 for budget reasons
- Optimal sales timing: **Year-round** (less seasonal dependency)

**Short Courses / Micro-credentials:**
- Rolling enrollment (often monthly or quarterly)
- Less seasonal impact
- Optimal sales timing: **Year-round with focus on Q1 and Q3**

**Community Services Qualifications:**
- Similar pattern to Cert IV/Diploma
- Additional: SIRTISS reporting cycles (quarterly) may create staff capacity variations
- Optimal sales timing: **April-June, October-November**
